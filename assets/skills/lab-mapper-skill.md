# Lab Mapper Skill

## Purpose

Lab Mapper Skill is designed for recruiting research talents from university lab pages, advisor homepages, and group member lists.

It turns a lab or advisor URL into a structured candidate mapping table, reducing repetitive manual search work in campus recruiting and research talent sourcing.

## Typical Inputs

- Lab homepage URL
- Advisor homepage URL
- Research group member page
- Target graduation year or degree level
- Optional target fields such as advisor, lab, email, homepage, Google Scholar, awards, and experience notes

## Typical Outputs

- Candidate name
- Degree level
- Advisor
- Lab or research group
- Enrollment year
- Estimated graduation year
- Personal homepage
- Google Scholar URL
- Email
- Awards and experience notes
- Short candidate judgment

## Workflow

1. Start from the direct lab or advisor page.
2. Prefer member links found on the official lab homepage.
3. Visit each student's personal page before using external search.
4. Extract CV, bio, education, publication, and contact information.
5. Infer enrollment and graduation year from self-descriptions.
6. Write results into a reusable mapping table.

## Use Case

Used for mapping 500+ students from relevant 2027 graduating labs and improving the speed of a previously manual recruiting workflow.
